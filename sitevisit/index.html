Artisan Manifesto

Beauty in Imperfection

Wabi-Sabi Philosophy

The Art of Slow Living

In our studio, we do not chase factory perfection. Every finger trace on clay, every glaze irregularity is a signature of the moment. We create items that carry the warmth of human hands and tell a story.

“Clay remembers every touch. It does not tolerate haste, but rewards patience.” — Oleg Demidov, Founder
The Artisan

Oleg Demidov

12 years behind the potter's wheel. Trained in Kyoto, Japan, studying traditional high-temperature anagama wood-firing techniques.

Materials

Pure Organics

We use only wild clay, coarse chamotte, and custom glazes crafted from wood ash and metal oxides.

Production

The Journey of Clay

01

Shaping

A meditative dialogue with spinning clay on the wheel or slab handbuilding.

02

Drying

Slow moisture evaporation in a controlled room for 7–10 days to prevent warping.

03

Bisque Firing

First firing at 950°C, making the clay strong yet porous enough to absorb glaze.

04

Glazing

Coating with custom glass formula and final glaze firing at 1220°C to lock in texture.
